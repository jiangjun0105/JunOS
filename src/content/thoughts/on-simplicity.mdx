# On Simplicity

Simplicity is not the starting point — it is the destination. You arrive at it
after discarding everything that is not load-bearing.

## The trap

The first version of anything is usually complex because you haven't yet
understood the problem well enough to make it simple. Premature simplicity is
just ignorance wearing a clean interface.

## The test

If you can remove a piece and nothing breaks, it wasn't simple — it was
incomplete. True simplicity is when every remaining piece is necessary and
sufficient.

## A rule I keep

> When I look at code I wrote six months ago and it feels obvious, that's
> simplicity. When it feels clever, that's a warning.
